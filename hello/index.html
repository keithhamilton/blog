Hello, World.

10 April 2015

Hi. I'm Keith.

If you've been to my site, you already know about me. If you have not been there, you should jump over to check it out.

I'm reasonably certain about some things.

I'm 35. I'm definitely not a life expert, nor am I really an expert in anything else. I am the quintessential "Jack of all trades," which is good, but also makes me less-than-entirely certain about some of the things I say. I'm not an expert. But I'm reasonably certain about some things.

I use the term reasonable certainty as a guard.

I am not prescient. I am not a know-it-all. In fact, if you knew me, one thing you would know is that above all else, I am completely convinced that I am most likely wrong about everything. That being said, I am reasonably certain about a slice of everything; obviously, this is the basis of opinion.

For example, take a solid. Like that hardwood table you spent your last bonus on. Shit hurts when you bang your head into it. It's solid. But it's not.

Take the sunset. The sun doesn't set. We rotate.

Take tau. Does nature calculate the irrational each time it makes a bubble on the surface of sea foam? Hmm.

Take water. Ah, now there's something logical. Each and every single time a water molecule is formed, it consists of precisely two hydrogen atoms, and a single oxygen atom. That sounds like something a pattern like nature would decide upon.

But we have all these constructs. Notions. Ideas about our world that are, for lack of a more descriptive term, patently false. That should cause alarm in the faithful, but it won't.

I got off track.

Anyway, this is my blog. Yay, I've joined the world. Read it and weep. Seriously, you will probably weep if you read this shit. You may do better to jump back to xkcd and pick a random comic strip.

--K
